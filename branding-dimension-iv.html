360 Degree Model

As markosis was about to build a website for Dimension IV, both client & the team decided it’s better to develop the brand along the way. For which Markosis applied 360 Degree Model that consists of the major stages to look into, to turn the brand around. The purpose was to increase return on investment, customer lifetime value and customer retention rate.

#1 Brand Identity

While conducting industrial research, Markosis wanted to look into Dimension IVs market share, brand exposure and types of communication. After which, Markosis decided to rebuild the brand identity, starting with the brand logo. After following a proper guideline, they build the logo, fixed the types of font, colours and design structure while incorporating them on the website.

#2 Brand Archetype

After establishing the brand identity, Markosis wanted to establish the brand archetype of Dimension IV. Dimension IV is a team of potential experts and youths in Hobart, Tasmania giving caring hands to its people. Markosis decided for the brand to become a Caregiver, an archetype that gives compassion and generosity and derives meaning from helping others.

#3 Buyer Persona

The third stage is where the team built up the buyer persona of Dimension IV based on the local business geography in Hobart, Tasmania. The buyer persona consisted of middle aged couples with the dream to build a life with financial freedom. That’s how Markosis built a 6 stage buyer persona for Dimension IV to understand who their targeted audience are.

#4 Brand Communication

After establishing a brand persona, Markosis went for the brand communication strategy. Since the buyer persona is middle aged adults related to families and the brand archetypes are made for caregiver, Markosis applied the sense into the communication strategy, mainly in marketing messages and content marketing strategies. The same strategy was applied on the website, which helped Dimension IV get more conversion within the shortest amount of time.
